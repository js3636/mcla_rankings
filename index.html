The 10-Method Composite

Each method is normalized 0–1 then combined with weights calibrated toward historically validated predictive accuracy in paired-comparison sports models.

Weight Rationale

Elo and Massey lead the weighting because they capture both quality of wins and recency simultaneously. Markov Chain and Bradley-Terry add transitivity robustness — "beating a team that beat a great team" flows upstream. Pythagorean Expectation corrects for luck: teams dramatically outperforming their goal differential tend to regress. SOS and OSOS carry low weight because they're partially redundant with the matrix-based methods (Colley and Massey already encode schedule difficulty intrinsically).

About This Project

MCLA 2026 Power Rankings is a computer-generated ranking system for Men's Collegiate Lacrosse built in R and published as a static website. All 468 games played in the 2026 season are scraped from mcla.us and processed through 10 independent statistical rating systems.

The composite score combines Elo ratings, Massey least-squares, Colley Matrix, Adjusted Margin of Victory, Markov Chain random walk, Bradley-Terry maximum likelihood, Pythagorean Expectation, Win Quality Score, Strength of Schedule, and Opponents' SOS into a single 0–100 power score.

Rankings are updated weekly during the season by running the R script locally and replacing the data in this file.

Data Sources & Methodology

Game data scraped from mcla.us covering all 173 teams across DI, DII, and DIII.

Home field advantage: +50 Elo points / 1.5 goal adjustment in AMoV. Margins capped at ±20 goals to prevent blowout inflation. Elo K-factor scales with margin and recency (later games weighted higher).

All rating components are normalized to [0,1] before weighting. The Bradley-Terry MM algorithm iterates until convergence (tol = 1e-6). Massey uses ridge regression regularization (λ = 0.01) to handle sparse early-season data.
